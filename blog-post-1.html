Concrete vs. Pavers for Hawaii Driveways

MaterialsJune 2, 2026 · By Derek Makua

Choosing between poured concrete and pavers for your Hawaii Island driveway is one of the most common decisions homeowners face. Both have genuine advantages, and the right choice depends on your property, budget, aesthetic preferences, and how you use your driveway. As Kailua-Kona's concrete specialists, we've built both — here's our honest comparison.

Upfront Cost

For a standard residential driveway, poured concrete typically costs 20–40% less than pavers. Concrete driveways on the Big Island run roughly $8–12 per square foot installed, while quality pavers run $15–25 per square foot or more. For a 1,000 square foot driveway, that's a difference of $7,000–$13,000. If budget is a primary factor, concrete wins decisively.

Aesthetics and Customization

This is where pavers traditionally had an advantage — but stamped concrete has largely closed that gap. Modern stamped concrete can replicate cobblestone, slate, flagstone, brick, and dozens of other patterns. With color hardener and release agent finishes, you can achieve virtually any color combination. Pavers do offer more visual variety in terms of genuine texture variation, but for most homeowners, stamped concrete is indistinguishable from pavers at normal viewing distances.

Durability in Hawaii's Climate

This is where concrete has a clear advantage on the Big Island. Hawaii's UV radiation is among the most intense in the United States. Pavers can fade and shift over time, and the joints between pavers can allow weed growth and ant intrusion — both significant issues in our tropical climate. Properly poured and sealed concrete maintains its appearance and structural integrity far better under Hawaii's sun and rain conditions.

Our Recommendation

For most Big Island homeowners, stamped or colored concrete gives you the best of both worlds — the aesthetic appeal of pavers at the durability and cost advantage of concrete.
