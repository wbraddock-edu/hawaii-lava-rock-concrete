FAQ

Common questions about our concrete and masonry services.

Do you offer free estimates?+
Yes! All estimates are completely free. We schedule an on-site visit to assess your project, take measurements, and provide a detailed written quote within 2–3 business days.
How long do concrete projects typically take?+
A standard residential driveway takes 2–4 days (pour + cure time). Patios and pool decks are similar. Large foundations and retaining walls may take 1–3 weeks depending on complexity and size.
Can you work with Hawaii's volcanic soil?+
Absolutely — this is one of our core specialties. We assess lava tube risk, soil composition, and drainage before any foundation or retaining wall work. Our designs account for Hawaii Island's unique geological conditions.
Do you need permits for concrete work in Kona?+
Many projects require Hawaii County permits, especially foundations, retaining walls over 4 feet, and anything near property lines. We handle all permit applications as part of our project management.
What's the difference between standard and stamped concrete?+
Standard concrete is a single gray finish. Stamped concrete uses patterns pressed into the surface while wet, creating the appearance of stone, brick, slate, or custom designs. Color hardener and release agents add color and depth.
How do I maintain my concrete in Hawaii's climate?+
Hawaii's intense UV, heavy rain, and salt air are hard on concrete. We recommend sealing every 2–3 years with a UV-resistant sealer. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners. Pressure washing annually helps, but use a wide-angle tip to prevent surface damage.
What causes concrete to crack in Hawaii?+
The most common causes are inadequate sub-base preparation, improper curing in our heat, moisture infiltration, and thermal expansion. We prevent cracking with proper control joints, reinforcement, and Hawaii-specific mix designs.
Are you licensed and insured in Hawaii?+
Yes. We hold an active Hawaii State C-5 Concrete contractor license and carry full general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. We can provide certificates of insurance for any project.
